Why Provider Credentialing Is Important

Provider credentialing is an important and foundational piece of successful healthcare revenue cycle management. The process verifies the qualifications of medical professionals and enrolls them with the insurance payers and service organizations accepted and utilized by the practice.

It is essential that providers possess the appropriate qualifications to provide medical care and address state and federal regulatory requirements. The process involves verification of education, training, licensure, certifications, and other required credentials for providing patient care.

Credentialing Has Become More Complex & Time-Consuming

The challenges in medical credentialing are growing with changes in regulatory compliance, accreditation standards, medical specialties, and innovative new medical technologies. Insurance payers typically have different requirements, standards, and application processes for provider enrollment. Success is driven by meticulous, disciplined credentialing processes and expertise.
